# Account, Security, And Privacy

Last reviewed: 2026-07-15

Status: **Maintained user reference - tenant policy and edition behavior vary**

## Protect Your Account

- Use a unique password and the service's password-reset flow.
- Enable two-factor authentication or a passkey when your community offers it.
- Store backup codes separately and never share them with support.
- Sign out on shared devices and review active sessions where that control is
  available.
- Treat unexpected passkey, password-reset, email-change, or transfer prompts as
  suspicious.

The platform can use JWT access tokens, refresh tokens, TOTP, backup codes, and
WebAuthn/passkeys. These are credentials. Do not paste them into screenshots,
issues, chat, or support forms.

## Profile And Community Visibility

Your tenant can control which profile fields and modules are available. Review
privacy and notification settings before publishing contact details,
availability, location, skills, photos, documents, or safeguarding information.
Blocking and reporting are separate actions: blocking limits interaction;
reporting asks moderators to review a concern.

## Data Export And Account Deletion

Where provided, the data-export/DSAR workflow lets you request or retrieve a
copy of account data. The account-deletion workflow is destructive and may
require reauthentication and a final confirmation. Read the exact confirmation
shown by your edition; do not treat a request as complete until the service
confirms its status.

Legal, safeguarding, fraud-prevention, financial, or audit duties can affect
what a tenant must retain. Repository maintainers cannot override those local
decisions. Direct privacy-rights questions to the data-controller/contact shown
by your community.

## Identity And Safeguarding Evidence

Submit identity or safeguarding evidence only through the designated protected
workflow. Do not attach it to an ordinary message or public software issue.
Provider availability and review status are tenant-specific. A badge, pending
status, or provider session should not be inferred from route presence alone.

## Report A Security Or Privacy Problem

For an immediate account concern, use the tenant's support or administrator
route. For a software vulnerability, follow the repository's
[security policy](../../SECURITY.md). Do not exploit another account, tenant,
or production system to demonstrate a report.
