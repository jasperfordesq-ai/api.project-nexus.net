# Getting Started As A Member

Last reviewed: 2026-07-15

Status: **Maintained user reference - behavior varies by community**

## 1. Use Your Community's Link

Project NEXUS communities are separate tenants. Start from the link supplied by
your community instead of guessing a tenant slug or changing one in the URL.
The same email address may have different membership or permissions in
different communities.

If you were given an accessible-service link, use the canonical
`/{tenantSlug}/accessible` mount. `/alpha` is a legacy redirect and is not the
current address to bookmark or use as evidence.

## 2. Register Or Accept An Invitation

Registration policy is tenant-controlled. Your community may use open
registration, an invitation, administrator approval, email verification, or an
identity-verification step. Complete only the steps shown by the service and do
not send identity evidence through ordinary email or a public issue.

Possible outcomes include:

- immediate activation;
- email verification required;
- administrator review pending;
- an invitation or additional verification required; or
- rejection with a community-specific contact route.

## 3. Sign In

Use the email, password, and community shown on the sign-in page. If two-factor
authentication is enabled, complete the displayed challenge. Never share a
one-time code, backup code, refresh token, or passkey prompt with support.

Use the password-reset flow if you have forgotten your password. Repeatedly
retrying credentials can trigger rate limits. If the account is pending,
inactive, or rejected, contact the community administrator rather than creating
duplicate accounts.

## 4. Complete Onboarding

Required onboarding can include profile details, community rules, consent,
skills, availability, accessibility needs, or safeguarding steps. Required
steps and their order are community-specific. A feature may remain unavailable
until onboarding is complete.

## 5. Check Your Profile And Preferences

Review your name and contact details, privacy choices, language, notification
preferences, and any profile visibility controls. Use accurate information but
publish only what you are comfortable sharing with the intended community.

## 6. Start With A Low-Risk Task

Good first tasks are browsing listings, reading a group or event, viewing the
Help Centre, or checking your wallet history. Before confirming a transfer,
exchange, marketplace purchase, application, or account deletion, review the
recipient, amount, tenant, and final confirmation carefully.

## When Something Is Missing

Availability can depend on tenant feature switches, your role, membership
status, moderation state, or the edition in use. A hidden or forbidden feature
is not permission to call its API directly. Record the visible error and ask
your community administrator or use the in-product support route.
