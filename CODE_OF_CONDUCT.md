# Project NEXUS Community Code Of Conduct

Last reviewed: 2026-07-15

Status: **Maintained reference — contributor and community participation policy**

Project NEXUS welcomes constructive participation from people of different
backgrounds, identities, abilities, and levels of experience. Everyone who
participates in this repository is expected to help keep collaboration safe,
professional, accessible, and focused on the public-interest purpose of the
project.

## Expected Conduct

- communicate with respect, patience, and good faith;
- critique ideas, code, evidence, and decisions rather than people;
- make space for accessibility needs and different communication styles;
- protect personal, tenant, production-derived, security, and incident data;
- disclose relevant conflicts, third-party material, and AI assistance as
  required by [`CONTRIBUTOR_TERMS.md`](CONTRIBUTOR_TERMS.md);
- accept evidence-backed correction and distinguish opinion from verified
  project state; and
- follow maintainers' safety, scope, licensing, and repository-boundary
  instructions.

## Unacceptable Conduct

Harassment, threats, discriminatory or sexualized conduct, deliberate
misgendering, doxxing, intimidation, sustained disruption, retaliation, and
publishing another person's private information are not acceptable. Neither is
using project access to expose secrets, confidential data, vulnerabilities, or
production-derived records.

## Scope

This policy applies to repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
commits, documentation, project-managed communication spaces, and public
representation of the project. It also applies when a participant uses a
project role or access relationship outside those spaces.

## Reporting And Enforcement

For ordinary support or code-quality concerns, follow [`SUPPORT.md`](SUPPORT.md).
For a vulnerability or security-sensitive report, follow the private-reporting
rules in [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) and do not include exploit details,
credentials, or personal data in a public issue.

For conduct concerns, contact the repository owner or project steward through a
private channel available on their GitHub profile or an established project
channel. If no private route is available, open a minimal issue asking for a
private contact method without describing the sensitive incident publicly.

The project steward may remove content, close or lock discussions, reject
contributions, restrict participation, or refer serious threats or unlawful
conduct to the relevant platform or authorities. Reports should be handled as
confidentially as reasonably possible, with conflicts of interest disclosed and
the subject of a report not deciding their own case.

No contributor, reporter, or reviewer may retaliate against a good-faith report
or participation in an investigation.
